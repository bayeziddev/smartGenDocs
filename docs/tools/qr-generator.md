## 1. What is a QR Code?

A QR (Quick Response) code is a two-dimensional barcode that can store various types of information. When scanned with a smartphone camera or a QR code reader app, it can instantly perform actions like opening a website, displaying text, connecting to Wi-Fi, or saving contact details. Their versatility makes them invaluable for marketing, business, and personal use.

## 4. Tips for Effective QR Code Usage

To maximize the impact of your QR codes, consider these best practices:

*   **Clear Call to Action**: Tell users what to expect when they scan the QR code (e.g., "Scan to visit our website," "Scan for Wi-Fi access").
*   **Optimal Size**: Ensure the QR code is large enough to be easily scanned from the intended distance. A general rule of thumb is that the size of the QR code should be at least 1 inch (2.5 cm) per 10 feet (3 meters) of scanning distance.
*   **High Contrast**: Use high contrast between the QR code and its background (e.g., black on white) for reliable scanning.
*   **Test Thoroughly**: Always test your QR codes with multiple devices and QR code scanner apps before deployment.
*   **Placement**: Place QR codes in easily accessible locations where users have enough time and space to scan them.
*   **Track Performance**: If possible, use tracking URLs to monitor how many scans your QR codes receive, providing valuable insights into your campaigns.
